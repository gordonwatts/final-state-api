## Analyses organized by the final state containing the long-lived particle `a`

The diagrams all produce a long-lived particle `a`, but they correspond to different **visible final-state topologies** depending on how the rest of the event decays. Here is a useful way to group them into search analyses.

---

### 1. **Jets + displaced `a` from top-rich final states**

**Diagram type:**  

- Gluon-initiated production leading to a top-quark pair, with one top decaying to `u/c + a` and the other decaying hadronically or semileptonically via `Wb`.

**Typical visible final state:**

- Multiple jets
- At least one `b`-jet
- Possibly a lepton + missing energy if the `W` decays leptonically
- Displaced/long-lived `a` signature

**Relevant analysis categories:**

- **Displaced vertex + jets**
- **Displaced hadronic resonance**
- **Jets + b-tags + displaced object**
- **Single-lepton + jets + displaced vertex** (if `W → ℓν`)

---

### 2. **Top-quark pair production with one top decaying to `a`**

**Diagram type:**  

- `g g → t t̄`, with one top modified to decay as `t → u/c + a`

**Typical visible final state:**

- Top-pair-like event
- One top gives standard `bW`
- The other gives a light jet + long-lived `a`
- Final states can be:
  - all-hadronic
  - lepton + jets
  - dileptonic

**Relevant analysis categories:**

- **Exotic top decay searches**
- **Displaced vertex in top-pair events**
- **Multijet + displaced signature**
- **Lepton + jets with displaced decay**

---

### 3. **Single-top–like production with a displaced `a`**

**Diagram type:**  

- `g u/c → t`-mediated process with an off-shell top, producing a final-state `u/c` or `t` plus `a`

**Typical visible final state:**

- One top quark plus one light jet
- Long-lived `a`
- Depending on the decay of the top:
  - `bW + jet + a`
  - with or without isolated lepton and missing energy

**Relevant analysis categories:**

- **Single-top + displaced vertex**
- **Jet + top + displaced object**
- **Lepton + jets + displaced decay**
- **Heavy flavor + displaced signatures**

---

### 4. **Flavor-changing top production with `a` in the final state**

**Diagram type:**  

- A process where a light quark `u/c` transitions through a top-quark line and emits `a`, giving a flavor-changing neutral-current-like topology

**Typical visible final state:**

- One top quark
- One light jet
- Long-lived `a`
- Possibly additional jets from top decay

**Relevant analysis categories:**

- **FCNC top searches with displaced decays**
- **Top + jet + displaced vertex**
- **Exotic quark flavor transition searches**
- **Hadronic displaced resonance searches**

---

## Summary table

| Diagram topology | Main visible final state | Search/analysis type |
|---|---|---|
| `gg → tt̄`, one top → `u/c + a` | jets, possibly leptons, `b`-jets, displaced `a` | displaced vertex + top events |
| `gg → tt̄` with exotic top decay | multijet or lepton+jets + displaced object | exotic top decay search |
| single-top–like with `a` emission | top + light jet + displaced `a` | single-top + displaced vertex |
| FCNC-like top/light-quark transition | top + jet + displaced object | FCNC top search |

---

## Practical experimental groupings

If you were sorting these into real collider analyses, the most natural buckets would be:

1. **Displaced vertices + jets**
2. **Displaced vertices + top quarks**
3. **Top-pair exotic decay searches**
4. **Single-top / FCNC top + displaced signature**
5. **Lepton + jets + displaced decay**  
   (when the `W` from top decay is leptonic)
