## Analyses organized by final state for LLP `a`

The diagrams correspond to production of a long-lived particle `a` in association with top quarks, often through a flavor-changing top interaction such as \(t \to u/c + a\). A sensible search program can be organized by the visible final state accompanying the LLP.

---

## 1. \(t\bar t\) production with one exotic top decay

### Diagram

\[
pp \to t\bar t,\qquad t \to u/c + a,\qquad \bar t \to bW
\]

with the shown case

\[
W \to j j'
\]

### Final state

\[
a + j_{u/c} + b + j + j'
\]

where \(a\) is long-lived.

### Analysis category

### **Hadronic \(t\bar t + a\) search**

Target final state:

- one LLP candidate from \(a\),
- one light-flavor or charm jet from \(t \to u/c+a\),
- one \(b\)-tagged jet,
- two additional jets reconstructing a hadronic \(W\),
- a reconstructed hadronic top from \(bjj\).

Possible LLP signatures from `a`:

- displaced vertex,
- displaced hadronic jet,
- displaced lepton pair,
- trackless or delayed jet,
- missing transverse momentum if `a` escapes the detector.

Typical selection handles:

- high jet multiplicity,
- one \(b\)-tag,
- reconstruction of \(W \to jj\),
- reconstruction of the SM top \(t \to bjj\),
- displaced object or anomalous missing energy from `a`.

---

## 2. Single-top production in association with an LLP

### Diagrams

\[
u/c + g \to t + a
\]

or related \(t\)-channel-like production with an incoming \(u/c\) quark and a gluon.

### Final state before top decay

\[
t + a
\]

The top quark then decays through

\[
t \to bW
\]

so the observable final state depends on the \(W\) decay.

---

## 3. Leptonic single-top + LLP search

### Final state

\[
a + b + \ell + \nu
\]

with

\[
t \to bW,\qquad W \to \ell\nu,\qquad \ell = e,\mu
\]

### Analysis category

### **Leptonic \(t+a\) search**

Target final state:

- one isolated electron or muon,
- missing transverse momentum from the neutrino,
- one \(b\)-tagged jet,
- one LLP candidate from `a`.

Possible LLP signatures:

- displaced vertex,
- displaced lepton pair,
- displaced jet,
- delayed calorimeter or muon-system activity,
- additional missing transverse momentum if `a` is detector-stable.

Typical selection handles:

- exactly one prompt lepton,
- one \(b\)-tagged jet,
- transverse mass compatible with \(W \to \ell\nu\),
- reconstructed or partially reconstructed leptonic top,
- displaced or invisible LLP signature.

---

## 4. Hadronic single-top + LLP search

### Final state

\[
a + b + j + j'
\]

with

\[
t \to bW,\qquad W \to jj'
\]

### Analysis category

### **Hadronic \(t+a\) search**

Target final state:

- one LLP candidate from `a`,
- one \(b\)-tagged jet,
- two light jets reconstructing the hadronic \(W\),
- reconstructed hadronic top \(t \to bjj\).

Possible LLP signatures:

- displaced hadronic decay,
- displaced multi-track vertex,
- trackless jet,
- delayed jet,
- missing transverse momentum from an invisible or detector-stable `a`.

Typical selection handles:

- at least three jets,
- one \(b\)-tag,
- \(m_{jj}\) compatible with the \(W\) mass,
- \(m_{bjj}\) compatible with the top mass,
- LLP object or large missing transverse momentum.

---

## 5. \(t\bar t\) semileptonic variant with exotic top decay

Although the displayed \(t\bar t\) diagram shows the SM top decaying hadronically, the same production mode also motivates a semileptonic analysis.

### Process

\[
pp \to t\bar t,\qquad t \to u/c+a,\qquad \bar t \to bW,\qquad W \to \ell\nu
\]

### Final state

\[
a + j_{u/c} + b + \ell + \nu
\]

### Analysis category

### **Semileptonic \(t\bar t + a\) search**

Target final state:

- one prompt lepton,
- one \(b\)-tagged jet,
- one additional light or charm jet,
- missing transverse momentum from the neutrino,
- one LLP candidate from `a`.

Typical selection handles:

- one isolated electron or muon,
- one \(b\)-tagged jet,
- at least one additional non-\(b\) jet,
- leptonic top reconstruction,
- displaced or invisible LLP signature.

## LLP-specific subcategories

Each of the above analyses can be further split depending on how the long-lived particle `a` appears in the detector:

| `a` behavior | Search signature |
|---|---|
| `a` decays to hadrons inside tracker | displaced hadronic vertex, displaced jets |
| `a` decays to leptons | displaced dilepton or lepton-jet search |
| `a` decays in calorimeter or muon system | delayed jet, displaced calorimeter cluster, muon-system vertex |
| `a` is detector-stable or decays outside detector | missing transverse momentum, possibly with top-associated objects |
| `a` is charged or leaves anomalous ionization | heavy stable charged particle-style search |

Thus the diagrams naturally motivate searches in hadronic and leptonic top final states, each combined with a displaced or invisible LLP signature from `a`.
